# Marking Scheme: End-Semester Examination (60 Marks)

## Q1. (2 marks) - Nature and Scope of Management
**Model Answer (points):**
- Management is a continuous process of planning, organizing, directing, and controlling.
- It involves coordinating resources to achieve organizational goals efficiently and effectively.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Definition of process: 1
- Explanation of functions: 1

## Q2. (2 marks) - Classical Management Approaches
**Model Answer (points):**
- Scientific Management focuses on individual task efficiency (Taylor).
- Administrative Principles focus on the overall organizational structure (Fayol).

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Scientific focus: 1
- Administrative focus: 1

## Q3. (2 marks) - Planning
**Model Answer (points):**
- Provides direction and reduces uncertainty.
- Establishes standards for controlling and minimizes waste.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Direction/Uncertainty: 1
- Standards/Waste: 1

## Q4. (2 marks) - Management by Objectives (MBO)
**Model Answer (points):**
- Set specific goals collaboratively between managers and employees.
- Monitor progress regularly and provide feedback for performance improvement.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Collaborative goal setting: 1
- Monitoring and feedback: 1

## Q5. (2 marks) - Leadership
**Model Answer (points):**
- Autocratic styles may lead to short-term productivity but lower morale.
- Democratic styles foster engagement and long-term commitment.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Autocratic impact: 1
- Democratic impact: 1

## Q6. (2 marks) - Controlling
**Model Answer (points):**
- Planning sets the targets (benchmarks).
- Controlling measures actual performance against these targets to take corrective action.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Planning as benchmark: 1
- Controlling as corrective measure: 1

## Q7. (2 marks) - Managerial Roles
**Model Answer (points):**
- Monitor: Seeking and receiving information.
- Disseminator and Spokesperson: Transmitting information to others inside and outside the unit.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Monitor role: 1
- Disseminator/Spokesperson: 1

## Q8. (2 marks) - Managerial Skills
**Model Answer (points):**
- Technical skills: Proficiency in specific tasks.
- Human skills: Ability to work with people.
- Conceptual skills: Ability to see the organization as a whole.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Listing skills: 1
- Brief explanation: 1

## Q9. (2 marks) - Organizational Structure
**Model Answer (points):**
- Matrix allows for better resource sharing across projects.
- Functional structures may become too rigid to handle cross-departmental project needs.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Resource sharing: 1
- Flexibility: 1

## Q10. (2 marks) - Organizational Culture
**Model Answer (points):**
- Shared values and beliefs within an organization.
- It influences employee behavior and organizational effectiveness.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Definition: 1
- Significance: 1

## Q11. (5 marks) - Evolution of Management
**Model Answer (points):**
- Scientific Management: Focus on work study and standardization.
- Administrative Theory: Focus on 14 principles of management.
- Bureaucratic Model: Focus on hierarchy and rules.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Scientific contribution: 2
- Administrative contribution: 2
- Bureaucratic contribution: 1

## Q12. (5 marks) - Planning
**Model Answer (points):**
- Setting objectives.
- Developing premises.
- Identifying alternatives.
- Evaluating alternatives and selecting the best.
- Implementation and follow-up.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Steps 1-3: 3
- Steps 4-5: 2

## Q13. (5 marks) - Motivation
**Model Answer (points):**
- Motivation leads to higher productivity and job satisfaction.
- It reduces employee turnover and absenteeism.
- Example: Offering performance-based bonuses to increase sales targets.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Importance points: 3
- Example: 2

## Q14. (5 marks) - Decisional Roles
**Model Answer (points):**
- Entrepreneur: Initiating change.
- Disturbance Handler: Managing conflicts.
- Resource Allocator: Distributing organizational resources.
- Negotiator: Representing the organization in deals.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Listing roles: 3
- Brief description: 2

## Q15. (10 marks) - Organizational Design
**Model Answer (points):**
- Functional: Grouped by specialty; efficient but siloed.
- Divisional: Grouped by product/market; autonomous but duplicate resources.
- Matrix: Dual reporting; flexible but complex.
- Comparison table or detailed analysis provided.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Functional description: 3
- Divisional description: 3
- Matrix description: 3
- Comparison: 1

## Q16. (10 marks) - Controlling
**Model Answer (points):**
- Establish standards (e.g., production quotas).
- Measure performance (e.g., daily output reports).
- Compare performance against standards.
- Take corrective action (e.g., maintenance or training).
- Justification: Ensures quality and efficiency.

**Marking Breakdown:**
- Steps of control: 6
- Justification: 4
